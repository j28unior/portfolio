§ 01 — Profile

A scholar who writes code,
a coder who writes papers.

Two professions, one practice. The classroom and the IDE inform each other.

I am a Computer Science lecturer, software developer, and academic researcher based in Gauteng, South Africa. My work sits squarely at the intersection of higher-education teaching and applied software engineering — a vantage point that informs both how I teach and what I research.

My academic trajectory runs from a Diploma and Advanced Diploma at the University of Johannesburg, through a BSc Honours at UNISA, an MCom in Information Systems and Technology at the University of KwaZulu-Natal, and into a PhD in Information Systems at UNISA. My doctoral research focuses on software development practices, Agile methodologies, and the role of AI in shaping modern engineering teams — practical work that draws on every classroom and every codebase I've worked in.

"I write modules the way I write software — opinionated, testable, and shaped by what students actually need on the other side of graduation."

I've designed and delivered modules in C#, ASP.NET, MVC, Microsoft Azure, React Native, R, Application Security, and Research Methods across both contact and distance modalities. I supervise honours research, sit on industry-engagement panels, and serve as an external moderator at UJ. My published work spans AI literacy in maritime education, ethical generative AI in academia, and the impact of AI on Computer Science curriculum design.

What I'm building next: a coherent body of work — modules, papers, and software — that makes a credible case for South African and African scholarship in software engineering education and information-systems research.
